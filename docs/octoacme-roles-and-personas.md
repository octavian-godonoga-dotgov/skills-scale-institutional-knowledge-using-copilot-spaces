# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Quality Assurance (QA) Specialists

### Role Summary
QA Specialists ensure that software meets quality standards and acceptance criteria before release. They design test strategies, execute tests, validate features against requirements, and collaborate with developers to identify and resolve defects.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain test plans and test cases
- Execute manual and automated tests for new features and regressions
- Validate features against acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Report, track, and verify bug fixes
- Collaborate with developers on testability and quality improvements
- Participate in sprint planning to understand testing requirements

### Goals
- Ensure features meet quality standards before release
- Reduce production defects and customer-reported issues
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Advocate for quality throughout the development lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report testing progress and blockers
- Bug reports and test result summaries
- Acceptance sign-off for completed features
- Collaboration with developers on test coverage and defect triage

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test coverage, testability, and defect resolution
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and validate feature behavior
- **Project Managers**: Report testing progress, risks, and quality metrics
- **Business Analysts**: Validate requirements implementation and edge cases
- **UX/UI Designers**: Test user flows and interface behavior

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create user-centered designs that ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and visually appealing. They conduct user research, design interfaces, create prototypes, and validate designs through user testing.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and create user personas
- Design wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Ensure design consistency and accessibility standards
- Validate designs through usability testing
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Maintain design systems and component libraries

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Validate designs with real users before development

### Typical Communication
- Design critiques and review sessions
- Usability testing reports and user feedback summaries
- Design handoff documentation and specifications
- Collaboration sessions with product and engineering

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align designs with product vision and user needs
- **Developers**: Collaborate on implementation and design feasibility
- **QA Specialists**: Partner on usability testing and UI validation
- **Business Analysts**: Translate requirements into user-centered designs
- **Technical Writers**: Ensure UI supports clear documentation and help content

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They elicit, analyze, and document requirements, ensuring that delivered features solve the right problems and deliver business value.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze workflows and identify improvement opportunities
- Create functional specifications and process flows
- Facilitate requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Validate that delivered features meet business needs
- Support User Acceptance Testing (UAT) coordination

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and testable requirements
- Align technical solutions with business objectives
- Reduce rework from unclear or changing requirements
- Facilitate effective stakeholder communication

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and specifications
- Stakeholder workshops and elicitation sessions
- Business process diagrams and workflow documentation
- UAT coordination and validation reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on requirements and prioritization
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and answer implementation questions
- **Project Managers**: Support planning with requirements and estimates
- **QA Specialists**: Define test scenarios and acceptance criteria
- **UX/UI Designers**: Ensure designs meet business requirements
- **Sponsor/Executive Stakeholders**: Present requirements and validate solutions

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create clear, accurate documentation that helps users understand and effectively use products and features. They produce user guides, API documentation, release notes, and knowledge base articles.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user documentation and help content
- Create API documentation and developer guides
- Draft release notes and change logs
- Ensure documentation accuracy and consistency
- Collaborate with developers on technical details
- Organize and maintain documentation repositories

### Goals
- Deliver clear, accurate, and accessible documentation
- Reduce support burden through comprehensive self-service content
- Ensure documentation stays current with product changes
- Maintain consistent documentation style and quality

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with subject matter experts
- Release notes and change summaries
- Collaboration with developers on technical accuracy
- Feedback sessions with support and customer success teams

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Source technical information and validate accuracy
- **Product Managers**: Understand feature purpose and user benefits
- **Project Managers**: Align documentation deadlines with releases
- **UX/UI Designers**: Ensure documentation aligns with user experience
- **QA Specialists**: Validate documentation against actual behavior

---

## Sponsor/Executive Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Sponsors and Executive Stakeholders provide strategic direction, funding approval, and organizational support for projects. They make go/no-go decisions, prioritize resources, and ensure projects align with business strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Approve project initiation and resource allocation
- Provide strategic guidance and business context
- Make high-level prioritization decisions
- Review and approve major milestones and deliverables
- Remove organizational blockers and escalations
- Champion project outcomes to leadership

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver strategic business value
- Optimize resource allocation across the portfolio
- Maintain alignment with organizational priorities
- Support teams in achieving project success

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or milestone-based status updates
- Business case reviews and ROI assessments
- Go/no-go decision meetings
- Escalation discussions for high-impact issues

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Receive status updates and escalations; provide decisions
- **Product Managers**: Align on strategic priorities and business outcomes
- **Business Analysts**: Review requirements and validate business value
- **Developers**: Provide context on business priorities (occasionally)
- **All Roles**: Remove organizational barriers and provide support
